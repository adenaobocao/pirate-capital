How a Tick Works one tick, five moves, every hour. no magic, no hidden steps.

a tick is one full round of decisions. the ship runs one every hour:

  1. real prices.
    daily candles for twelve stock token tickers. the prices are always real, whatever era the chests are in.
  2. a thousand voyages.
    for each ticker we run a monte carlo simulation: 1,000 possible five day futures. it turns a chart into odds: expected return, chance of ending up, how ugly the worst 5% gets.
  3. the tavern.
    24 simulated investors (a dividend boomer, an options degen, a skeptical quant...) argue about every ticker. their mood becomes a sentiment score per name. it is noisy on purpose, because markets are.
  4. the crew decides.
    each pirate reads the same brief with its own philosophy and answers with orders: buy, sell or hold, plus the reasoning you see in the log. hold counts as a decision.
  5. the articles.
    risk rules written in code, signed like ship articles: max position size, max orders per tick, max spend per buy. the ai never gets to break them.

hold is a position. most good ticks are boring ticks.
